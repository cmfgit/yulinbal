35-Minute Lesson Plan

Unit: Environmental Change and Management

Inquiry Question: What can we do to protect our environment?

Focus: Drawing & Research Activities

5 minutes

Introduction & Hook

  • Show slides 1-3: Introduction to human environmental impacts
  • Visual Hook: Display dramatic before/after photos of environmental change
  • Quick poll: "Have you noticed environmental changes in your local area?"
  • Introduce today's activities: drawing and research
8 minutes

Direct Instruction & Case Study

  • Present slides 4-5: Great Barrier Reef case study & food webs
  • Explain interconnections in ecosystems using food web example
  • Discuss: "What happens if one part of the food web disappears?"
  • Introduce drawing task: students will create their own food web
12 minutes

Drawing Activity (Choice of 2)

Option 1: Food Web Sketch

  • Draw an Australian ecosystem food web (forest, reef, or desert)
  • Include at least 6 organisms with arrows showing energy flow
  • Label producers, consumers, and decomposers

Option 2: Before & After Environmental Change

  • Draw two scenes side-by-side
  • Left: Environment with human impact (pollution, deforestation, etc.)
  • Right: Same environment after conservation efforts
  • Add labels explaining the changes

Teacher circulates, provides feedback, helps with ideas

7 minutes

Research Task

  • Students use devices/textbooks to research 3-4 questions from research sheet
  • Focus on finding specific facts, numbers, and real examples
  • Encourage use of reliable sources (.gov, .edu, .org websites)
  • Students record sources for their information

Research Tips:

  • Use search terms like "Australia + [topic] + statistics"
  • Look for recent articles (last 2-3 years)
  • Write answers in your own words
3 minutes

Share & Reflect

  • Gallery walk: Students display drawings on desks
  • Quick share: 2-3 students share one interesting research finding
  • Class discussion: "What was the most surprising thing you learned?"
  • Exit ticket: "Name one action YOU will take to help the environment"

üé® Drawing Activities

Complete ONE of these drawing tasks (or both for extra credit!)

Activity 1: Australian Ecosystem Food Web

üìù Instructions:

  1. Choose an Australian ecosystem: Rainforest, Reef, Desert, or Grassland
  2. Research or recall organisms that live in that ecosystem
  3. Draw at least 6 different organisms
  4. Use arrows to show energy flow (who eats whom)
  5. Label each organism and its role:
    • üåø Producer (plants)
    • ü¶ò Primary Consumer (herbivores)
    • ü¶Ö Secondary Consumer (carnivores)
    • üçÑ Decomposer (breaks down dead matter)

üí° Tips for Success:

  • Start with the sun at the top (energy source)
  • Arrows point FROM prey TO predator (energy direction)
  • Make your drawings clear and colorful
  • Add a title: "Food Web of [Your Ecosystem]"

üåü Example Organisms:

Rainforest:

  • Trees, ferns
  • Insects, tree frogs
  • Snakes, owls
  • Fungi, bacteria

Reef:

  • Algae, seagrass
  • Small fish, sea turtles
  • Sharks, barracuda
  • Bacteria
Draw your food web here (or use separate paper)
Activity 2: Before & After Environmental Restoration

üìù Instructions:

  1. Divide your page into TWO equal sections (left and right)
  2. LEFT SIDE - "BEFORE": Draw an environment damaged by human impact
    • Show specific problems: pollution, deforestation, litter, etc.
    • Use darker colors to show damage
  3. RIGHT SIDE - "AFTER": Draw the same place after conservation efforts
    • Show solutions: clean water, replanted trees, wildlife return
    • Use brighter colors to show improvement
  4. Add labels explaining what changed and how

üéØ Scenario Ideas:

River Restoration:

  • Before: Polluted, murky water, dead fish, trash
  • After: Clean water, fish swimming, plants growing

Forest Regrowth:

  • Before: Cleared land, tree stumps, eroded soil
  • After: New trees, animals returned, healthy soil

‚ú® Extension Challenge:

Add a third section showing "FUTURE" - what could this environment look like in 10 years with continued protection?

Draw your before & after scenes here (or use separate paper)
