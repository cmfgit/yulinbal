About This Portfolio

This is an in-class and take-home assessment. Through your portfolio you will demonstrate understanding of anatomy and biomechanics in a real sporting context.

3 / 4 Question 1

The Muscular & Skeletal Systems 10 marks

Choose ONE sporting movement (e.g. throwing a ball, kicking, jumping, swimming stroke) and use a diagram or image to address all three parts below.

🦴 Part A — Label

  • Identify at least 4 bones involved in the movement
  • Identify at least 4 muscles involved in the movement
  • Clearly label these on a diagram or annotated image

📝 Part B — Describe

  • For each bone and muscle labelled, write 2–3 sentences describing its role during the movement
  • Explain what the muscle/bone does at each phase (e.g. propulsion, support)

🔄 Part C — Joint Type

  • Identify the type of joint most critical to the movement (e.g. ball-and-socket, hinge)
  • State the range of motion it allows
  • Explain why this joint type suits the movement

💡 Tip: Choose a movement you are familiar with. The more specific your descriptions, the higher your marks. Use correct anatomical terminology throughout.

4 / 4 Question 2

Muscles & Bones Working Together 8 marks

Using the same sporting movement from Q1, explain how the muscular and skeletal systems work together to produce efficient movement.

💪 Part A — Muscle Roles

For one phase of your movement, identify:

  • Agonist — the prime mover producing the force
  • Antagonist — the opposing muscle that controls movement
  • Stabiliser(s) — muscles that fix joints for control

⚙️ Part B — Lever System

Explain the lever system at work:

  • State the class of lever (1st, 2nd, or 3rd)
  • Identify the fulcrum (joint), effort (muscle force) & load (resistance)
  • Describe the mechanical advantage this provides

🏃 Part C — Efficient Movement

Bring it all together:

  • Explain how the agonist/antagonist relationship produces smooth, controlled movement
  • Describe how the skeletal lever system amplifies force or speed
  • Link to overall performance efficiency

💡 Tip: Use a clearly labelled diagram of the lever system for Part B. Most movements in sport use a 3rd class lever — prioritise speed and range of motion over mechanical advantage.
