Question 1

DNA is shaped like a twisted ladder. What is this shape called?

A

Double helix

B

Single strand

Answer: A

Double helix

DNA is shaped like a twisted ladder - this is called a "double helix". The two sides are made of sugar and phosphate, while the rungs are base pairs (A-T and C-G).

Question 2

In DNA, which base always pairs with Thymine (T)?

A

Cytosine (C)

B

Adenine (A)

Answer: B

Adenine (A)

DNA bases always pair in specific ways: A pairs with T, and C pairs with G. This is called complementary base pairing!

Question 3

How much DNA is inside one human cell?

A

About 2 centimetres

B

About 2 metres

Answer: B

About 2 metres

Each cell contains approximately 2 metres of DNA! It fits inside tiny cells because DNA is tightly packaged and coiled around proteins called histones.

Question 4

When DNA is tightly packaged, what structures does it form?

A

Chromosomes

B

Ribosomes

Answer: A

Chromosomes

Chromosomes are tightly packaged bundles of DNA wrapped around histone proteins. Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs) in most cells.

Question 5 - New Concept!

Where do proteins come from in your body?

A

They are made using instructions from genes

B

They only come from food we eat

Answer: A

They are made using instructions from genes

Your body makes its own proteins using instructions stored in your genes! Each gene is a section of DNA that contains the code for making one specific protein.

Question 6 - New Concept!

What is the first step in making a protein from a gene?

A

The protein appears immediately

B

The gene's DNA code is copied into RNA

Answer: B

The gene's DNA code is copied into RNA

Making a protein is a two-step process. First, DNA is copied into RNA (transcription). Think of it like making a photocopy of instructions before using them!

Question 7 - New Concept!

Why does the cell make an RNA copy instead of using DNA directly?

A

To protect the original DNA from damage

B

Because DNA cannot make proteins

Answer: A

To protect the original DNA from damage

DNA is your master instruction manual and stays safely in the nucleus. Making an RNA copy is like photocopying a recipe - you can use the copy without risking damage to the original!
