What is Genotype?

üìñ Definition: Genotype

Genotype is the actual genes (alleles) an organism has.

It's like the instruction manual written in your DNA - you can't see it just by looking at someone!

Examples of Genotypes:

‚Ä¢ For eye color: BB or Bb or bb

‚Ä¢ For height: TT or Tt or tt

‚Ä¢ These letters represent the alleles (versions of genes) you inherited

üìñ Definition: Phenotype

Dominant & Recessive Traits

üîç Dominant Traits

A dominant trait shows up even if you only have ONE copy of the gene.

‚Ä¢ Written with a CAPITAL letter (like B)

‚Ä¢ Only need 1 copy to see the trait!

üîç Recessive Traits

A recessive trait only shows up if you have TWO copies of the gene.

‚Ä¢ Written with a lowercase letter (like b)

‚Ä¢ Need 2 copies to see the trait!

Example: Eye Color

‚Ä¢ B = brown eyes (DOMINANT)  |  b = blue eyes (RECESSIVE)

Possible Genotypes and Phenotypes:

‚Ä¢ BB ‚Üí Brown eyes (2 dominant)

‚Ä¢ Bb ‚Üí Brown eyes (1 dominant is enough!)

‚Ä¢ bb ‚Üí Blue eyes (2 recessive needed)

ü©∏ Special Example

Blood Groups - A Special Case

Why Blood Groups are Different

Blood groups have THREE possible alleles instead of just two!

‚Ä¢ IA = codes for type A

‚Ä¢ IB = codes for type B

‚Ä¢ i = codes for type O (recessive)

Special Rules:
1.IA and IB are both DOMINANT over i
2.IA and IB are CO-DOMINANT (both show up together)
3.i is RECESSIVE (needs 2 copies to show)

What is Co-dominance?

When you have both IA and IB, BOTH show up!

This creates blood type AB - a phenotype showing BOTH traits!

üí° Worked Examples

Blood Group Examples Explained

Example 1: Type A Blood

Phenotype: Type A blood (observed in test)

Genotype options: IAIA or IAi

Why both work: IA is dominant, so it masks i

Example 2: Type AB Blood

Phenotype: Type AB blood

Genotype: IAIB - ONLY ONE possible genotype!

Why both show: Co-dominance means BOTH alleles appear!

Example 3: Type O Blood

Phenotype: Type O blood

Genotype: ii - ONLY ONE possible genotype!

Why special: Must have TWO recessive alleles - no dominant ones!

What We Learned Today

Understanding genotype and phenotype helps us predict traits in offspring, explain why family members look similar (but not identical), and understand how genetic diseases are inherited. Blood groups show us that genetics can be more complex than simple dominant/recessive patterns!

üåü Key Takeaways

  • Genotype: Genes you have (hidden) vs Phenotype: Traits you see
  • Dominant: Shows with 1 copy (capital) vs Recessive: Needs 2 copies (lowercase)
  • Blood groups show multiple alleles and co-dominance
  • Different genotypes can create the same phenotype
