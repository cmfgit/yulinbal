Dopamine is a chemical messenger (neurotransmitter) made by nerve cells in your brain. It is released when something feels rewarding ‚Äî like eating food you love, winning a game, getting a like on social media, or hearing music you enjoy.

Dopamine travels across a tiny gap between two brain cells called a synapse and attaches to a receptor on the other side ‚Äî sending a signal that says: "That felt good ‚Äî do it again!"
üîë Analogy: Think of dopamine like a text message your brain sends to itself. The receptor is like your phone ‚Äî if it works well, the message gets through clearly. If your phone (receptor) is faulty or your signal (dopamine) is too low, the message gets lost.
